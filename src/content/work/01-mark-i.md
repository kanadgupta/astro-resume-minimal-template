---
title: Research Fellow, Computation Project
company: Harvard University
period: Jul 1944 - Jun 1949
---

- Served as one of the first three programmers of the Harvard Mark I, computing critical ballistics and mathematical tables for the U.S. Navy during World War II.
- Authored the 561-page _Manual of Operation for the Automatic Sequence Controlled Calculator_, one of the earliest comprehensive references on programming a digital computer.
- Programmed the Mark II and Mark III and logged the original computer “bug,” coining terminology still used across software engineering today.

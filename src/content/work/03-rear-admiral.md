---
title: Director, Navy Programming Languages Group
company: U.S. Navy
period: Aug 1967 - Aug 1986
---

- Recalled to active duty to standardize the Navy’s high-level programming languages and compilers across the fleet.
- Drove definition and validation of COBOL standards and compiler conformance, unifying business computing across the Department of Defense.
- Developed compiler validation suites that certified consistent language behavior across dozens of vendor systems.
- Directed the Navy Programming Languages Group, coaching officers and engineers on software standards and long-term maintainability.
- Championed distributed networks of small computers over centralized mainframes, anticipating modern systems architecture.
- Delivered hundreds of lectures on computing efficiency and innovation to military, industry, and academic audiences.
- Retired as Rear Admiral in 1986, then the oldest serving officer in the U.S. Navy, and received the Defense Distinguished Service Medal.
